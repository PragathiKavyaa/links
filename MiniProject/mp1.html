About

A portfolio highlighting A.P.J. Abdul Kalam's contributions would showcase his journey from a humble beginning to becoming a renowned aerospace scientist, the 11th President of India, and a source of inspiration. It would include details on his education, his work on India's space and missile programs, his time as President, and his legacy of inspiring youth.

Education

Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, on October 15, 1931. Early life involved selling newspapers to support his family. Studied physics and aerospace engineering.

Education

Joined DRDO (Defence Research and Development Organisation) in 1958. Played a key role in developing India's space and missile programs, earning him the title "Missile Man of India". Led the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program, developing missiles like Agni and Prithvi. Also involved in the development of launch vehicles and satellite technology.

Presidency

Elected as the 11th President of India. Known as the "People's President" for his down-to-earth demeanor and ability to connect with ordinary people. Served as a moral and intellectual authority, inspiring the nation with his vision and leadership.

Awards

Bharat Ratna (1997), India's highest civilian award. Padma Vibhushan (1990) and Padma Bhushan (1981). Numerous honorary doctorates and awards from various universities and institutions.

Personal Life

Remained a bachelor throughout his life. Passionate about India and its development. Dedicated his life to science, technology, and service to the nation.

Death

Died on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management in Shillong. His death was a great loss to the nation, as he was a source of inspiration and a symbol of national pride.
